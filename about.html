Welcome,

I am a postdoctoral fellow working in the D’Esposito Cognitive Neuroscience Lab at the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute, UC Berkeley. My research focuses on brain network mechanisms of cognitive control. I also have a strong interest in researching how brain connectivity develops from childhood to adolescence.

Before coming to Berkeley, I received my PhD in Cognitive Psychology and Cognitive Neuroscience from the University of Pittsburgh, working with Dr. Beatriz Luna. During my PhD study, I was a member of the Center for the Neural Basis of Cognition, a joint research training program between Pitt and CMU.

Before that, I was a Master's student with Dr. Ralph-Axel Mueller at San Diego State University. I obtained my bachelor's degree from National Chung Cheng University, Taiwan.

I am funded by an NIH NRSA F32 postdoctoral fellowship.
