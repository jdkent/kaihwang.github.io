A collection of useful papers

Here I will try to update and maintain a collection of papers that I found usefull or interesting.


This is a nice paper on how to do non-parametric statistics on neuroimaging data. Though written for electrophysiology data, I found the principle applicable to other types of neuroimaging data or metrics derived from imaging data. In my experience, non-parametric approach is more flexible.
Maris, E., & Oostenveld, R. (2007). Nonparametric statistical testing of EEG-and MEG-data. Journal of neuroscience methods, 164(1), 177-190.



Many concepts that moderm graph theoretic analyses on brain networks can be traced back to these calssic papers:
Mesulam, M. (1990). Large‐scale neurocognitive networks and distributed processing for attention, language, and memory. Annals of neurology, 28(5), 597-613.

Goldman-Rakic, P. S. (1988). Topography of cognition: parallel distributed networks in primate association cortex. Annual review of neuroscience, 11(1), 137-156.

Selemon, L. D., & Goldman-Rakic, P. S. (1988). Common cortical and subcortical targets of the dorsolateral prefrontal and posterior parietal cortices in the rhesus monkey: evidence for a distributed neural network subserving spatially guided behavior. The Journal of Neuroscience, 8(11), 4049-4068.
