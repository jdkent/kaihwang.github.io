Kai Hwang, PhD

Cognitive Neuroscience

University of California, Berkeley

I am a postdoctoral fellow working in the D’Esposito Cognitive Neuroscience Lab at the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute, UC Berkeley. My current research investigates brain network dynamics involved in information selection, enhancement, and inhibition. Together these mechanisms support flexible information processing in the brain, and are particularly important to attention, memory, and response inhibition. I also have a strong interest in understanding how network brain network dynamics develop from childhood to adolescence to support cognitive development.
You can learn more about my work by at the "research" or "publication" page.
