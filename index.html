Kai Hwang, PhD

Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute

University of California, Berkeley



I am a postdoctoral fellow working in the D’Esposito Cognitive Neuroscience Lab at the Helen Wills Neuroscience Institute, UC Berkeley. My current research investigates brain network dynamics involved in information selection, enhancement, and inhibition. Together these mechanisms support flexible information processing in the brain, and are particularly important to attention, memory, and response inhibition. One main focus is how thalamocortical interactions are involved in these processes. I also have a strong interest in understanding how network brain network dynamics develop from childhood to adolescence to support cognitive development.

I received my PhD in Cognitive Psychology and Cognitive Neuroscience from the University of Pittsburgh, working with Dr. Beatriz Luna.

During my PhD study, I was a member of the Center for the Neural Basis of Cognition, a joint research training program between Pitt and CMU.

Before that, I was a Master's student with Dr. Ralph-Axel Mueller at San Diego State University.


I was born and was raised in Taiwan.

You can learn more about my work by visiting the "research" or "publication" page.
