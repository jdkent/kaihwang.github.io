Research

I am broadly interested in brain network mechanisms of cognitive control and its developmental process. Specifically, I study how neural pathways provide a substrate for generating flexible and adaptive behaviors, and how these systems develop. Currently, one major focus is the thalamocortical system and its relation to cognitive control, cortical network interactions, and development. My research utilizes a variety research methods, including fMRI, TMS, MEG, task-free (resting-state) and task-based functional connectivity, graph analyses, oculomotor paradigms, and studies of patients with focal cortical or subcortical lesions.
